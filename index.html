Standardized GMDSS Testing Protocols

Mandatory routine maintenance and operational verification procedures for critical maritime communication equipment to ensure safety of life at sea.

Daily Tests

  • Verify internal connections, display, and power output of VHF DSC. Run built-in test (no signal transmission).
  • Execute built-in diagnostic on MF/HF DSC without external transmission.
  • Confirm proper INMARSAT-C login status to the correct Ocean Region.
  • Check reserve battery charge status and verify printer paper availability.

Weekly Tests

  • Conduct DSC test call on approved HF channels to Coast Stations.
  • Verify internal selective calling between duplicate VHF sets (low power).
  • Manually switch off main supply to verify operation on reserve test batteries (if applicable).
  • If no coast station acknowledges, document a vessel-to-vessel test call.

Monthly Tests

  • Run INMARSAT-C Performance Verification (PV) and test SSAS protocols.
  • Perform EPIRB self-test (without breaking seal) and inspect HRU conditions.
  • Visually examine AIS/X-Band SARTs. Conduct live test avoiding interference with port operations.
  • Examine emergency battery cells thoroughly and check handheld VHF batteries.

Annual & Logbook

  • Comprehensive Annual Inspection conducted by certified radio surveyors.
  • Logbook Rule: Every entry must include precise vessel position (Lat/Long).
  • Record both Local and UTC time for all tests, failures, and repairs.
  • Primary responsibility for testing typically falls to the designated radio officer (often 2nd Officer).

Testing Best Practice

For distant stations, conduct MF/HF tests post-sunset or pre-sunrise to capitalize on optimal ionospheric wave propagation.
